A little about me

I am an aerospace engineering student specialising in structures and materials for space, and aerospace has held my attention for as long as I can remember. What sustains it is the central question of structural engineering: not only whether a design works, but precisely why, and whether it will hold under the conditions it was built for. Structures and materials are where my curiosity is strongest, and pursuing that interest is what brought me to Delft.

I created this site because a CV and a profile capture only a fraction of who someone is. Alongside engineering, I am someone who has painted since childhood, who is happiest outdoors and working with her hands, and who is consistently drawn to teaching and to working alongside others. This page is intended to offer a fuller picture than a list of titles and grades can — both the engineer and the person behind it.

Academic Merit Award 2023/24 and 2024/25

Integrated Project — Fixed-Wing VTOL UAV for Medical Logistics

2024/25

The conceptual design of a fixed-wing VTOL drone for delivering medical supplies in regions with limited ground infrastructure, built around a case study of Vancouver Island, Canada. Using a hybrid propulsion system, the aircraft was designed for both urgent long-range deliveries to isolated communities and frequent short-range transfers between healthcare facilities. The configuration was selected through the Analytic Hierarchy Process and refined with iterative aerodynamic, stability and structural analysis to ensure airworthiness. The most substantial project of my degree, and the one I consider my strongest piece of undergraduate work. Large file (~10 MB).

Flight Control — Longitudinal Control System for a Mirage 2000

2024/25

(originally in Portuguese)

The design, analysis and simulation of a longitudinal flight control system. The work progressed from the state-space formulation of the aircraft’s open-loop short-period and phugoid dynamics, through a root-locus stability augmentation system, to a Linear Quadratic Regulator for optimal velocity and climb-angle tracking. The controller’s robustness was then validated against real-world constraints — sensor noise, time delays, and actuator rate and saturation limits — during a dynamic terrain-following mission.

2024/25

(originally in Portuguese)

The processing and analysis of experimental flight data across three areas: Fast Fourier Transform analysis to isolate the frequency content of multi-component accelerometer signals; a custom peak-valley algorithm to count fatigue load cycles from a Dornier-Dassault Alpha Jet; and an evaluation of an EGNOS/GPS satellite navigation system against ICAO civil-aviation standards, including the analysis of protection levels and the isolation of integrity-violation events.

Work

What I've been building

Structures Engineer — Da Vinci Satellite
Mar 2026 – Present
ESA “Fly Your Satellite!”

I work on the structural verification of the Da Vinci satellite, dedicating around eight hours per week to ensuring its structure can withstand launch. My responsibilities include static and modal FEM analyses of the payload components, and the development of a simplified whole-satellite model to support system-level structural verification ahead of submission to ESA. Contributing to mission hardware intended for orbit is, for me, the clearest expression of why I chose this discipline.

Teaching Assistant — Wind Energy MOOC
Apr 2026 – Present
TU Delft — Teach-the-Teacher programme

I contribute to the development of TU Delft’s first Teach-the-Teacher course on wind energy, dedicating around six to eight hours per week to writing storyboards, designing content templates, and sourcing visual and animation references with the academic lead. The course is intended to give primary and secondary school teachers the resources to introduce wind energy into their classrooms, an objective I find particularly worthwhile, given the importance of engaging young people with renewable energy.

Teaching Assistant & Course Moderator — Aerospace Structures MOOC
Mar 2026 – Present

I dedicate roughly two to four hours per week to supporting more than 2,000 learners worldwide on the Introduction to Aerospace Structures and Materials MOOC, moderating technical discussions and resolving conceptual questions. The experience has reinforced my view that the quality of teaching directly determines whether students persist with difficult material.

Structural Design & Prototyping — AeroTéc
2023 – 2024

It was my first real experience of taking an aircraft from concept to something that genuinely flew, and the moment it first left the ground was another confirmation that this is the work I want to dedicate myself to.

Volunteering

Just a Change 2024

Just a Change is a Portuguese NGO that renovates and rebuilds homes for families living in inadequate or unsafe housing conditions, with volunteers carrying out the construction work directly. In the summer of 2024 I took part in a one-month programme rebuilding a house in a village in the district of Coimbra, and I subsequently continued to support the organisation’s projects in the district of Lisbon. The work is physically demanding and entirely practical, and it captures something central to how I like to contribute: directly, with my hands, on something that makes a concrete difference to the people it serves.
